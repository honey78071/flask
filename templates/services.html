Thoughts...

What if we called them all (both chips and cards) .card(s) then have a modifier that determines size and styles. Of course the difference in the cards would be markup and the style controlled by the modifier. For example: .card, .card .card-small, or .card .card-button.

Chips have a max-width of 500px, is this needed? Should this be controlled based on the grid? Cards have a width of 100% however they should never be in a full width grid.

Card Inventory

  1. Standard cards with image, title, description: offerings
  2. Large, vertical, with author and small button: blog
  3. Simple cards with extended buttons: home, events (image, title, button)
  4. Simple cards: store (image, title, price)
  5. Price cards: store items (button, price) similar to the single event card which is not a card but we need an option (perhaps not a card class) to add the price
  6. Description cards no title: bottom of resources (image, description)
  7. smaller, horizontal cards (aka chip) with date or author: resources found at top of resources

Card Classes and Usage

  • Card: (1, 2, 4, 6) .card Image, must have either title and/or description, author (optional), date (optional), small button (optional)
  • Card with Full Button: (3, 5) .card .card-button-full Difference is the title is centered (optional) and the button is full width
  • Card Small: (7) .card .card-small different layout, contains image, title, short description (date or author)
